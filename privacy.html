Privacy Policy

At EyeRest, we believe your health and your data should stay in your control. This Privacy Policy outlines how the EyeRest Chrome extension operates and why we collect absolutely zero personal data.

The "Zero-Data" Promise: EyeRest is built with a privacy-first architecture. We do not use any servers, analytics, or third-party tracking. Everything happens locally on your computer.

1. No Data Collection

EyeRest does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information or usage data. This include, but is not limited to:

  • Your browsing history or habits.
  • Your geolocation or IP address.
  • Your email address or identity.
  • Usage metrics (e.g., how often you take breaks).

2. Permissions Explained

The EyeRest extension requests only the minimum number of permissions required to function. Here is exactly why we need them:

  • Alarms: Used to run the 20-20-20 timer accurately in the background without draining your battery.
  • Storage: Used to save your custom settings (break interval, duration, and theme) locally on your device.
  • Notifications: Used to send you a gentle desktop nudge when it's time for a break.
  • Tabs: Used to open the exercise screen in a new window during your rest period.

3. Local Operation

All your preferences (such as break time and exercise settings) are stored within your browser's local storage engine (chrome.storage.local). This data is never synced to any cloud and disappears the moment you uninstall the extension.

4. Third-Party Links

Our landing page and extension may contain links to our official GitHub repository. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of GitHub or any other third-party websites you may visit through these links.

5. Open Source Transparency

To provide total peace of mind, EyeRest is completely open source. You or any security professional can review the full source code on GitHub to verify that no tracking or data harvesting exists in the codebase.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or the security of the extension, you can open an issue on our GitHub repository or contact us directly at our development office.
